## Method

Three instruments, escalating, because the first under-counted:

1. **`solidstats-parser-rust-code-review` pass** (3 per-crate agents: core / worker / contract+cli+quality).
   Result: "a few findings" — because a code-review lens applies the §G noise filter and collapses a
   systemic divergence into ONE finding ("also at lines …"). Good for signal, wrong for sizing edit-surface.
2. **Firsthand mechanical probes** (orchestrator): suppression inventory, determinism/totality greps,
   CI/gate state, structural pass (dep graph + module trees).
3. **Divergence-hunt Workflow** (`parser-conformance-divergence-hunt`, 12 hunters + completeness critic):
   the OPPOSITE of a review — per convention rule, enumerate EVERY divergent site, no noise filter, no
   collapsing, raw-divergence vs genuine-exception split, mechanical/semantic + behavior-risk tags.

The hunt is the authoritative sizing. The review + firsthand probes remain the semantic detail behind it.

## Completeness critic — the hunt UNDER-sized two areas (must fold into phases)

1. **§H Async & worker — no hunter owned this cluster.** Confirmed live divergences (some already
   seen by the worker code-review agent):
   - no `enable_auto_recover()` / `wait_for_recovery()` (amqp.rs ~146-167) → worker exits on a
     recoverable broker blip;
   - no S3 `operation_timeout` / `operation_attempt_timeout` (storage.rs `from_config` ~173-260) → a
     stalled read hangs the serial worker;
   - 3 bare unbounded `tokio::spawn` (runner.rs:67/76/258, health.rs:172) — none semaphore/JoinSet-bounded;
   - no `JoinSet`/`join_next` drain on shutdown (drain is via a counter, not task-drain);
   - amqp.rs:108 nacks with `requeue=true` → possible requeue-loop vs the "nack requeue=false to DLX" rule
     (cross-check: prior review classified poison→ack-after-publish as conformant; reconcile this).
   - COMPLIANT but uncredited: idempotency via S3 `if_none_match('*')` (storage.rs:294); prefetch=1 bounded.
   → **Add a §H cluster to the worker phase (P4).**
2. **§C determinism — 6 of 7 sub-rules unowned (only overflow-checks claimed).** Highest-consequence gap:
   **total-stable-ordering of 3 sort sites is unaudited** — `entities.rs:73` `sort_by(compare_entities)`,
   `entities.rs:187` + `aggregates.rs:162` `sort_by_key(source_entity_id)`. A non-total comparator here
   would silently desync the artifact run-to-run — exactly the §C determinism risk. Also: no epsilon
   helper for float compares (Confidence f32 at presence.rs:90). HashMap/IndexMap absence already
   confirmed clean. → **Add a §C sort-key total-ordering audit to the determinism work (P3).** This is
   behavior-critical: a latent non-total comparator is the one thing that could break golden-parity
   during the refactor.
